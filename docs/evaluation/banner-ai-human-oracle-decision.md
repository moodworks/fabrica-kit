## OCR scoring asymmetry

Person and text-heavy contain visible watermark regions whose use is permitted but whose content
remains unresolved. The oracle does not invent a watermark transcription and does not treat
unresolved content as exact oracle text. For those two fixtures:

- approved main-text recall and bounding-box accuracy at IoU ≥ 0.70 are scored;
- every actual observation and unmatched extra observation is retained in the result;
- extra-observation precision is `unavailable-unscored`, not silently passed or failed;
- unresolved extras are not silently promoted into exact oracle occurrences;
- full exact OCR eligibility is false and a full exact OCR pass is prohibited even when every
  approved main-text occurrence and box is perfect.

Product and no-text have complete visible-semantic-text oracles. Precision, recall, and box
accuracy are all scored. The product passes full scoring only with all five exact occurrences,
adequate boxes, and no extra semantic text. The no-text fixture passes only with an empty actual
set; every nonempty observation is counted explicitly as a semantic false positive and fails full
scoring.

Model confidence is never used as oracle truth.
